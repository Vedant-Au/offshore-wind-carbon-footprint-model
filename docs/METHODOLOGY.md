# Methodology

## Boundary

The model is cradle-to-gate: raw-material extraction, processing, component manufacturing, transportation and offshore setup. Operations, maintenance and end-of-life are outside the scenario boundary.

## Two analytical views

The impact table reports category intensities per kWh. For each category:

`manufacturing share = manufacturing / (manufacturing + plant setup)`

The turbine scenario uses a separate planning estimate:

`cradle-to-gate t CO2e = lifecycle t CO2e/MW × capacity MW × cradle-to-gate share`

At 528 t CO2e/MW and 15 MW, the lifecycle planning total is 7,920 t CO2e. Applying 70%-80% produces 5,544-6,336 t CO2e.

## Decision rule

Prioritise the stages with the greatest contribution, but validate technical feasibility, cost and supplier influence before implementation. The model indicates direction and sensitivity; it does not provide a verified product carbon footprint.
